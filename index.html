Platform & Technology

What does "Causal-Native" mean and why does it matter?

+
Causal-Native means the AI doesn't just identify correlations ("waste went up when it rained") it identifies cause-and-effect relationships at every stage of the production cycle. Using directed acyclic graphs (DAGs), our engine determines whether a waste event was caused by a prep error, a demand spike, an ordering mistake, or a holding-time failure. This enables counterfactual "what-if" analysis if we had reduced the batch by 15%, what would the GP margin have been? No other UK platform offers this capability.

How does the Recipe-State Digital Twin work in practice?

+
The Digital Twin treats every menu item as a probabilistic state machine modelling its transformation from raw ingredient (delivery) through preparation, cooking, holding, and service. At each stage, the Twin calculates yield probability and waste risk. If a chicken breast entering the prep stage has a 23% yield loss probability due to current holding conditions, the system alerts the kitchen team before the item reaches the bin identifying the "yield leakage" that costs UK institutions millions annually.

How does the platform operate without internet connectivity?

+
The Offline-First Edge AI architecture runs full causal inference locally on the Kitchen-Brain Edge hub sustaining over 95% functionality during network outages. This is critical for NHS rural hospitals, university halls, and high-pressure service periods. When connectivity resumes, the system synchronises anonymised operational data to the cloud, enabling continuous model improvement and cross-site learning without compromising data privacy or GDPR compliance.
